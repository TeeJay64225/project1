The Team

Empowered. Dedicated. Advocates.

Managing Partner

ELSIE APPAU

Elsie Appau, a Ghanaian lawyer and human resources expert, rose from humble beginnings in Sokode-Bagble, Ghana, to become a successful CEO. She has extensive experience in various sectors, including water governance, business sustainability, and youth development. Elsie has worked with organizations such as the Netherlands Foreign Affairs Ministry, UN agencies, World Bank, and African Development Bank. She is currently the Africa Region Representative of Acreaty Management Plc and President of the Women's Economic Forum - Ghana Chapter. Elsie serves on the boards of multiple corporate and non-governmental entities, advocating for women's economic empowerment and SME development. She holds numerous academic qualifications and has received several awards for her contributions. Elsie is also an entrepreneur with interests in real estate, fashion, hospitality, and management consultancy. She is a member of various professional bodies and has trained hundreds of business owners, particularly SMEs.

Executive Secretary

EFFIBA KOJUAH AMIHERE

Effiba Amihere is a legal practitioner admitted to the Ghana Bar in 1998, specializing in various legal fields. She began her career as a teaching assistant at the University of Cape Coast before transitioning to law. As a private practitioner, she has represented clients across diverse areas, initially focusing on cases involving women and children in distress. Her expertise extends to land litigation, trusts, and estates, gained through her tenure at Global Legal Consultancy and Sozo Law Consult, where she now serves as the managing partner. Effiba's firm, EFFIBA AMIHERE & ASSOCIATES, offers comprehensive legal services, including notary public and company secretarial roles. She specializes in construction law, providing legal support to government agencies, public companies, and various professionals involved in construction projects. Effiba is adept at contract negotiation, drafting, and review, as well as advising on environmental compliance. She also excels in corporate law, assisting in company incorporations, obtaining permits, and recovering debts for financial institutions. Her experience extends to advising municipal assemblies on administrative and local government laws, particularly regarding property rights and labor issues. In addition to her legal practice, Effiba serves as the Director, Legal, at the National Labour Commission, providing counsel on employment, labor, and collective bargaining matters. She is actively involved in human rights advocacy through the African Women Lawyers Association, advocating for legislation to support vulnerable groups in Ghana. Effiba's contributions extend beyond her practice; she has been a member of the Rules of Court Committee since 2005, shaping the legal landscape of Ghana.

Executive Member

FLORENCE CHRISTINA MAAAME HAGAN

Florence Christina Maame Hagan's educational journey took her through various institutions, including Ghana International School, Mfantsiman Girls Secondary School, the University of Ghana, Legon, and Warwick University. She holds degrees in English, Study of Religions, and Law, being called to the Bar in both the UK and Ghana. With over three decades of experience, Florence's expertise spans diverse legal areas, including Banking and Finance, Human Resource Development, Commercial Law, and Energy-related issues. She brings exceptional management and analytical skills to her roles and actively engages in professional and community service organizations. As the Managing Partner of Hagan Law Company, Florence leads a dynamic legal consulting firm focused on delivering tailored solutions and redefining legal services in Ghana. Additionally, she serves as a Notary Public and holds the position of District Governor Elect for Rotary International District 9104. Florence plays a pivotal role as the Country Coordinator for the Attorney General Alliance | Africa Programme (AGA Africa), formerly known as the Conference of Western Attorneys General. She is a member of several professional bodies, including the Ghana Bar Association, Ghana Arbitration Centre, and the African Women Lawyers Association (AWLA).

Executive Secretary

BETTY MOULD-IDDRISU

Betty Mould-Iddrisu boasts nearly four decades of legal expertise within the Ghanaian Bar, with a distinguished career encompassing academia, public service, civil society, and politics. She holds a Master's degree in Law (LLM) with a specialization in Intellectual Property Law from the London School of Economics & Political Science (LSE). Throughout her tenure at the Ghanaian Ministry of Justice, starting in 1980, etty held various roles, notably serving as Copyright Administrator of hana and Chief State Attorney in the Attorney-General’s Department. Her pioneering work in Intellectual Property Law earned her multiple awards and recognition across Africa. Internationally, Betty excelled as Director of the Legal and Constitutional Affairs Division of the Commonwealth Secretariat, overseeing the implementation of international law mandates from Commonwealth Heads of State and legal officials. Betty is celebrated for her advocacy in gender equality and human rights across English-speaking African countries. She chaired both Ghanaian and African Regional groups of the International Federation of Women Lawyers (FIDA) and co-founded the African Women Lawyers Association (AWLA). Her grassroots initiatives focused on empowering women and children in accessing justice and judicial reform garnered widespread acclaim and numerous awards. In academia, Betty lectured at the University of Ghana’s Law Faculty for over a decade, authored numerous papers, and served as a resource person at national, egional, and global legal forums. With over 20 years of consultancy experience in International Law, Intellectual Property Law, and multilateral trade issues for organizations like the UN, Betty's expertise is highly regarded. Her political career saw her become Ghana’s first female Attorney General and Minister for Justice in 2009, later serving as Minister of Education in 2011. Betty continues to play an active role in political leadership within Ghana.
